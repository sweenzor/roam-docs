Comments

markdown version · view in Roam Research

  • Comments let you and your collaborators discuss a block without editing it: questions, feedback, or side notes that stay out of the text itself.
  • Threads open in the Right Sidebar, so the conversation sits next to the thing it's about.

How to comment

  • Hold Cmd (Mac) or Ctrl (PC) and hover over a block, then click the + button that appears at its right edge.
  • Or run Comment on current block while your cursor is in the block:
    • Mac: Cmd-Alt-=
    • PC: Ctrl-Alt-=
  • Either way, the thread opens in the right sidebar with an empty comment ready for you to type.
  • Replies are just blocks: type more in the thread to continue the conversation.

Where comments live

  • Comments are ordinary blocks on the same page as the block they discuss. They collect in a collapsed roam/comments block at the bottom of the page, grouped by date and author, and each thread starts with a ((block ref)) to the block it's about.
  • Because of that reference, a commented block shows a reference count at its right edge, and its comment threads appear among its linked references.
  • Since comments are ordinary blocks, they sync, search, and export like the rest of your graph.

Good to know

  • Commenting needs edit access to the graph, so read-only visitors can't comment.
  • AI tools connected through Roam MCP or the Local MCP can read and add comments too, which lets an agent leave feedback without touching your text.
